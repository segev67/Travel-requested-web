Visit Tokyo

Tokyo is a bustling metropolis with a unique blend of traditional and modern culture. From ancient temples and shrines to cutting-edge technology and innovative cuisine, Tokyo has something to offer for every kind of traveler. Whether you're interested in history, food, or entertainment, Tokyo is a city that is sure to fascinate and inspire.

Top attractions

  • #1

    Shibuya Crossing

    Shibuya Crossing is one of the most famous intersections in the world, known for its busiest and most colorful street scene. The crossing is surrounded by giant video screens, trendy boutiques, and popular restaurants, making it a must-visit destination for anyone interested in Tokyo's vibrant pop culture. Visitors can take a walk around the popular Shibuya area, grab a coffee at one of the many cafes, and take in the sights and sounds of this lively neighborhood. At night, the crossing is especially spectacular, with thousands of pedestrians crossing at the same time, creating a dazzling display of lights and energy. The area is also home to the famous Hachiko statue, a loyal dog who waited for his owner at the station for years.

    Meiji Shrine

    The Meiji Shrine is a serene Shinto shrine located in a tranquil forested area, providing a peaceful escape from the hustle and bustle of the city. Dedicated to the deified spirits of Emperor Meiji and his wife, Empress Shoken, the shrine is a beautiful example of traditional Japanese architecture, with a stunning torii gate and a peaceful garden. Visitors can take a stroll through the peaceful grounds, visit the shrine's museum, and learn about the history and significance of the shrine. The shrine is also a popular spot for traditional Japanese weddings and festivals, making it a great place to experience Japanese culture. The surrounding Yoyogi Park is also a popular spot for cherry blossom viewing in the spring.

  • #3

    Tokyo Skytree

    The Tokyo Skytree is the tallest tower in the world, standing at an impressive 634 meters tall. The tower offers breathtaking views of the city from its observation decks, which are located at 350 and 450 meters high. Visitors can enjoy stunning panoramic views of Tokyo, including famous landmarks such as the Tokyo Tower, the Imperial Palace, and Mount Fuji on a clear day. The tower is also surrounded by a shopping and entertainment complex, with a variety of restaurants, shops, and attractions to explore. At night, the tower is illuminated with a stunning light show, making it a must-visit destination for anyone interested in Tokyo's vibrant cityscape.

    Tsukiji Outer Market

    The Tsukiji Outer Market is a bustling marketplace located next to the famous Tsukiji Fish Market, one of the largest fish markets in the world. The outer market is a great place to try some of Tokyo's freshest sushi and seafood, with a variety of restaurants and food stalls to choose from. Visitors can also browse the stalls selling everything from fresh produce to kitchenware, and take in the sights and sounds of this lively marketplace. The market is also a great place to learn about Japanese cuisine and culture, with many of the vendors offering samples and explanations of their products. The nearby Tsukiji Hongan-ji Temple is also a beautiful example of Japanese architecture, with a stunning main hall and peaceful gardens.

    Asakusa Kannon Temple

    The Asakusa Kannon Temple, also known as Senso-ji, is one of the oldest and most famous temples in Japan, with a history dating back to the 7th century. The temple is dedicated to the goddess of mercy, Kannon, and is famous for its giant paper lantern and Nakamise shopping street, which is filled with traditional snacks and souvenirs. Visitors can explore the temple's main hall, which is decorated with intricate carvings and paintings, and take a stroll through the peaceful gardens. The temple is also a great place to learn about Japanese history and culture, with many of the temple's buildings and artifacts dating back to the Edo period. The nearby Asakusa district is also a great place to experience traditional Japanese culture, with many traditional restaurants and shops to explore.
