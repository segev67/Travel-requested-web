Visit Tokyo

Tokyo is a bustling metropolis with a unique blend of traditional and modern culture. From ancient temples and shrines to cutting-edge technology and innovative cuisine, Tokyo has something to offer for every interest. With its vibrant atmosphere and rich history, Tokyo is a destination that will leave you fascinated and eager to explore.

Top attractions

  • #1

    Shibuya Crossing

    Shibuya Crossing is one of the most iconic landmarks in Tokyo, known for its busiest and most colorful street intersection in the world. The crossing is surrounded by giant video screens, trendy shops, and restaurants, making it a must-visit destination for anyone looking to experience the city's vibrant energy. Visitors can take a walk around the popular Shibuya area, grab a coffee at one of the local cafes, or simply people-watch from the famous scramble crossing. With its unique atmosphere and lively vibe, Shibuya Crossing is a great place to take in the sights and sounds of Tokyo. As the sun sets, the area transforms into a dazzling display of neon lights, making it a perfect spot for photography and souvenir shopping.

  • #2

    Meiji Shrine

    Meiji Shrine is a serene Shinto shrine located in a tranquil forested area, offering a peaceful escape from the hustle and bustle of the city. Dedicated to the deified spirits of Emperor Meiji and his wife, Empress Shoken, the shrine is famous for its beautiful gardens, traditional architecture, and peaceful atmosphere. Visitors can stroll through the peaceful forest, take part in traditional Shinto rituals, or simply relax in the serene surroundings. The shrine is especially popular during cherry blossom season, when the grounds are filled with vibrant pink blooms. With its stunning natural beauty and historical significance, Meiji Shrine is a must-visit destination for anyone interested in Japanese culture and history.

  • #3

    Tokyo Skytree

    Tokyo Skytree is the tallest tower in the world, offering breathtaking views of the city from its observation decks. At 634 meters tall, the tower provides an unparalleled perspective on Tokyo's sprawling metropolis, with vistas stretching as far as the eye can see. Visitors can enjoy shopping and dining at the tower's base, or take a lift to the observation decks for a panoramic view of the city. The tower is especially stunning at sunset, when the sky is painted with hues of pink and orange. With its cutting-edge technology and stunning views, Tokyo Skytree is a must-visit destination for anyone looking to experience the city from a unique perspective.

  • #4

    Tsukiji Outer Market

    Tsukiji Outer Market is a bustling marketplace where visitors can sample the freshest sushi and seafood in the city. While the inner market has moved to a new location, the outer market still offers a fascinating glimpse into Tokyo's seafood culture, with rows of stalls selling everything from fresh tuna to octopus. Visitors can take a stroll through the market, sample local delicacies, or try their hand at making sushi at one of the many restaurants and food stalls. The market is especially popular in the early morning, when the freshest seafood is available. With its lively atmosphere and incredible food options, Tsukiji Outer Market is a must-visit destination for any food lover.

    Asakusa Kannon Temple

    Asakusa Kannon Temple, also known as Senso-ji, is one of the oldest and most famous temples in Japan, offering a glimpse into the country's rich history and culture. The temple is dedicated to the goddess of mercy, Kannon, and is famous for its giant paper lantern, traditional Nakamise shopping street, and beautiful gardens. Visitors can explore the temple grounds, take part in traditional rituals, or simply admire the stunning architecture. The temple is especially popular during festivals and special events, when the grounds are filled with colorful decorations and lively performances. With its rich history and cultural significance, Asakusa Kannon Temple is a must-visit destination for anyone interested in Japanese heritage.
