Visit Prague

Prague, the capital of the Czech Republic, is a city steeped in history and culture. With its stunning architecture, vibrant arts scene, and rich heritage, Prague is a must-visit destination for any traveler. From the iconic Charles Bridge to the majestic Prague Castle, the city is filled with breathtaking landmarks and hidden gems waiting to be discovered.

Top attractions

  • #1

    Charles Bridge

    The Charles Bridge is a iconic landmark in Prague, spanning the Vltava River and connecting the Old Town to the Lesser Town. This medieval stone bridge is adorned with 30 statues of saints and is a popular spot for both tourists and locals. As you walk across the bridge, you can take in the stunning views of the river and the city's skyline, and admire the intricate stone carvings and Gothic architecture. The bridge is particularly beautiful at sunset, when the sky is painted with hues of pink and orange. Whether you're looking for a romantic stroll or a unique perspective on the city, the Charles Bridge is a must-visit attraction.

    Prague Castle

    The Prague Castle is a majestic complex of palaces, churches, and gardens that dominates the city's skyline. This historic fortress has been the seat of power for Bohemian kings, Holy Roman emperors, and Czech presidents, and is now home to the Czech Parliament and the Office of the President. As you explore the castle, you can marvel at the stunning architecture, visit the stunning St. Vitus Cathedral, and take in the breathtaking views of the city from the castle walls. The castle is also home to several museums, galleries, and gardens, making it a great place to learn about Czech history and culture. With its rich history and stunning architecture, the Prague Castle is a must-visit attraction for anyone interested in history, architecture, or culture.

    Old Town Square

    The Old Town Square is the heart of Prague's historic center, surrounded by stunning architecture, lively street performers, and bustling cafes. The square is dominated by the iconic Astronomical Clock, which dates back to the 15th century and is one of the most famous clocks in the world. As you explore the square, you can admire the stunning Gothic architecture of the surrounding buildings, visit the stunning St. Nicholas Church, and take in the lively atmosphere of the square. The square is also home to several street performers, including musicians, jugglers, and living statues, making it a great place to soak up the city's energy and atmosphere. With its rich history, stunning architecture, and lively atmosphere, the Old Town Square is a must-visit destination for anyone visiting Prague.
