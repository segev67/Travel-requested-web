Visit Tel Aviv

Tel Aviv is a vibrant and cosmopolitan city located on the Mediterranean coast of Israel. Known for its stunning beaches, rich history, and thriving cultural scene, Tel Aviv is a must-visit destination for any traveler. From its ancient ruins to its modern architecture, Tel Aviv has something to offer for everyone.

  • #1

    White City

    The White City is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and a must-visit attraction in Tel Aviv. This unique neighborhood is home to over 4,000 buildings designed in the Bauhaus style, making it one of the largest collections of Bauhaus architecture in the world. Visitors can take a guided tour to learn more about the history and significance of this iconic neighborhood. The White City is not only a testament to Tel Aviv's rich cultural heritage but also a thriving hub of artistic and cultural activity. With its stunning architecture, vibrant atmosphere, and rich history, the White City is a must-visit destination for anyone interested in architecture, design, or culture.

    Jaffa Old Town

    Jaffa Old Town is a historic neighborhood located in the southern part of Tel Aviv, and it's a must-visit destination for anyone interested in history, culture, or architecture. This ancient port city has been inhabited for thousands of years, and its narrow streets and alleys are filled with a range of historic landmarks, including ancient ruins, historic buildings, and picturesque courtyards. Visitors can explore the neighborhood on foot, taking in the sights and sounds of this vibrant and historic area, which is home to a range of artists, musicians, and craftspeople. Jaffa Old Town is also famous for its stunning views of the Mediterranean Sea, which can be enjoyed from a range of scenic lookout points and rooftop terraces. With its rich history, vibrant culture, and stunning natural beauty, Jaffa Old Town is a must-visit destination for anyone interested in exploring the best of Tel Aviv's historic heritage.
